Understanding Credit Cards 101

1. The Billing Cycle

Your statement is generated monthly on a fixed date. Transactions done within this cycle must be paid on or before the Due Date (usually 20 days post cycle statement date).

2. APR & Interest Charges

Annual Percentage Rate (APR) represents the yearly interest. If you carry a balance (even ₹1) past the due date, interest is calculated daily on all outstanding balances from the transaction date.

3. Credit Utilization

Using more than 30% of your credit limit raises flags to bureaus, hurting your score even if paid in full monthly.

💹 Investing Fundamentals

Master long-term asset allocation, diversification, ETFs, and mutual funds.

Asset Allocation & Diversification

Diversification is the ultimate risk management shield. Spreading capital across Stocks (Equities), Bonds (Fixed Income), Gold (Commodities), and Cash ensures portfolio stability during market stress.

Primary Investment Vehicles

ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)

Passive funds tracking index baskets (like S&P 500 or Nifty 50), trading directly on exchanges like normal stocks.

Mutual Funds

Pooled investment portfolios managed by professional fund managers, trading once daily at Net Asset Value (NAV).

REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

Trust companies owning rent-generating real estate, distributing 90% of taxable income to shareholders as dividends.
